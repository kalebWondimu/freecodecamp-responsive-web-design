Abebe Bikila born August 7, 1932 in the small village of Jato near Mount Choke, Shewa Province, Ethiopia. Abebe, who was raised in the countryside, worked as a shepherd in his youth, and lived a traditional Ethiopian life knowing what lay ahead.

At a young age Abebe joins the Imperial Guard of Ethiopia (an elite military unit responsible for protecting the Emperor Haile Selassie).He noticed in those years in the Guard that his athleticism started to show, in particular with distance running.

Abebe starts training at a serious level under coach Onni Niskanen who recognizes his natural endurance and running style. He competes in local races and military athletics events. He 's noticed for his speed and endurance.

September 10, 1960: Abebe Bikila stuns the world by winning the Olympic Marathon in Rome, running all the way without shoes.He wins gold in 2: 15: 16. 2, breaking the world record and becoming the first African to ever win an Olympic gold medal.His historic run by the Obelisk of Axum, a monument to Ethiopian heritage he says has been taken to Rome -- a symbolic return of Ethiopia's pride, he says.

Abebe becomes first person in history to win two Olympic marathons -- unprecedented at the time.

1968 ‚Äì The Final Olympic Run

Despite injuries, Abebe competes in the 1968 Mexico City Olympics. Unfortunately he is forced to withdraw at the 17 km finish line with a leg injury. Although he does not complete the race, he is still an inspiring example of persistence.

1969 ‚Äì Tragic Turn

As far as we know, Abebe is paralyzed from the waist down in a car accident in March 1969. The world mourns the evil turn of events but Abebe, always the fighter, lives with strength and dignity.

Abebe competes in table tennis and archery tournaments for athletes with disabilities in spite of his condition.He speaks as a motivational speaker and participates in a number of public events, reminding people that resiliency is the true mark of a champion.

1973: A Hero's Farewell

On October 25, 1973, Abebe Bikila dies at the age of 41 from complications resulting from his accident.The world and Ethiopia lament the passing of a legend. In Addis Ababa, he is laid to rest with full military honors. He is referred to as a national hero by Emperor Haile Selassie himself.
